Mars rovers

Mars rovers are motor vehicles that travel across the surface of the planet Mars upon arrival. Rovers have many advantages over stationary landers. They can examine more territory, they can be directed to interesting features, they can place themselves in sunny positions to weather winter months, and they can advance the knowledge of how to perform very remote robotic vehicle control.

Currently there are three active rovers on the surface of mars. The first one is Curiosity by NASA, which was launched on November 26th, 2011, and it landed at the Aeolis Palus, near Aeolis Mons. The second rover is Perseverance. It is a rover created by NASA and it was built upon the successful design of Curiosity. It was launched with the Mars 2020 mission on July 30th, 2020. It landed on February 18th, 2021. It also carried a little friend, a Mars Helicopter called Ingenuity. The third, and most recent, rover that is active on Mars is Zhurong. It was launched with the Tianwen-1 CNSA Mars mission on July 23rd. It dropped a remote selfie camera on June 1st, 2021.

Sadly, there are rovers on the surface of mars which are no longer active, like Mars 3, Sojourner, Spirit and Opportunity.
