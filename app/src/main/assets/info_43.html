Thrusters and reaction-control systems

If we want to keep a rocket on its calculated path, we need to have some kind of system put in place to have control over its attitude, that is its orientation in space. We can achieve this by using thrusters and reaction-control systems. Thrusters are low thrust “engines” which have control over the spacecraft’s attitude, these are also referred as reaction-control thrusters. Another method of achieving stabilization is to use electrically powered reaction wheels (or also called momentum wheels). These wheels are mounted in three orthogonal axes aboard the spacecraft. They provide a means to trade angular momentum back and forth between the spacecraft and wheels. If you want to rotate the spacecraft in a direction, you have to spin the wheel into the opposite direction.
