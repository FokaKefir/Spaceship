Solar panels

Solar panels, or also called a photo-voltaic modules, use the light energy from the Sun to generate electricity through the photovoltaic effect. The photovoltaic effect is the generation of voltage and electric current in a material upon exposure to light. This effect is closely related to the photoelectric effect, in which photons knock electrons out from their orbitals, becoming free electrons and thus creating an electric voltage potential difference.

There are three types of solar panels:

  1. Mono crystalline
  2. Poly crystalline
  3. Thin-film solar panels

Each of these types is made in a unique way and have a different appearance. Their efficiency varies from the type of the solar panel, but the best achieved solar module efficiency is around 21.5%.
