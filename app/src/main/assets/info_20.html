Black holes

Black holes are a region of space where the gravity is so strong that nothing (no particles, or even electromagnetic radiation) can escape from it. In every galaxyâ€™s core there is a supermassive black hole, which keeps the entire galaxy together, with the help of dark matter.

On the 10th of April 2019 the Event Horizon Telescope managed to photograph a black hole, this is the first image of a black hole ever captured. This black hole was the supermassive black hole at the core of supergiant elliptical galaxy Messier 87, with a mass about 7 billion times that of the Sun.
