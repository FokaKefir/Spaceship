Apollo program

The Apollo program, or also known as Project Apollo, was the third human spaceflight program carried out the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), which succeeded in preparing and landing the first humans on the Moon from 1968 to 1972.

The first crewed flight was Apollo 7 and saw resumption of human spaceflight by the agency after the fire that killed the three Apollo 1 astronauts during a launch rehearsal test on January 27, 1967.
