Galaxies

A galaxy is a gravitationally bound system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter. Galaxies are categorized by their visual appearance, they can be elliptical, spiral, or irregular.

The oldest galaxy ever discovered, as of March 2016, is GN-z11. It has a distance of 32 billion light-years from Earth, and it is seen as it existed about 400 million years after the Big Bang. This galaxy can be found in the constellation Ursa Major.
