Earth

Our planet, the Earth, is the third planet from the Sun in our Solar System. This is the only astronomical object known to support life. About 29.2% of Earth’s surface is land consisting of continents and islands, while the remaining 70.8% is covered with water. Earth’s polar regions are covered in ice. Earth was formed around 4 and a half billion years ago.

The outer layer is divided into several tectonic plates that migrate across the surface over many millions of years, while its interior remains active with a solid iron inner core and a liquid outer core, which is responsible for generating Earth’s magnetic field, and it’s also called the geomagnetic field. This magnetic field extends from the Earth’s interior out into space, where it interacts with solar wind, which is a stream of particles emanating from the Sun. Because of this, Earth’s magnetic field protects us from solar flares.
