NASA

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration is an independent agency o the U.S. federal government responsible for the civilian space program, as well as aeronautics and space research. NASA was established in 1958 by Dwight D. Eisenhower, succeeding the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics. It is headquartered in Washington, D.C., United States. Its slogan is “At NASA, we make Air and Space available for everyone” (April 6th, 2021).
