Speed of light

The speed of light in a vacuum, denoted “c” is a universal physical constant. This value is defined as 299.792.458 meters per second (or approximately 300.000 kilometres per second). Since light is an electromagnetic wave, we can say that electromagnetic waves travel around this speed. Although the speed of light is commonly associated with electromagnetic radiation, it is also the speed at which massless particles and field perturbations travel in a vacuum, including gravitational waves (yes, gravity is a wave as well).

Fun fact, it takes around 8 minutes and 20 seconds for the light to reach the Earth from the sun. This is because of the enormous distance between them, so by when you start seeing the Sun rise above the horizon, it is way above that.
