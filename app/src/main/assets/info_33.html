International Space Station

The International Space Station (or ISS) is a modular space station in low Earth orbit (LEO). It is a multinational collaborative project involving five participating space agencies: NASA, Roscosmos, JAXA, ESA and CSA. It was first launched into orbit on November 20th, 1998, at Site 81/23, Kennedy, Launch Complex 39 (LC-39).

Its length is 73 metres, it is 109 metres, its mass is around 440,725 kilograms. It follows an orbit with an apogee altitude of 422 kilometres, and a perigee altitude of 418 kilometres.

It is capable of supporting 7 astronauts when fully crewed.
